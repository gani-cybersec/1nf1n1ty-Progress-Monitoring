# Task 1: Introduction to Digital Forensics

## Overview

Forensics is the application of scientific methods to investigate crimes and establish facts. With the increasing use of computers, smartphones, and other digital devices, a specialized branch known as **digital forensics** emerged to investigate crimes involving digital evidence.

Digital forensics applies computer science techniques to identify, collect, preserve, analyze, and present digital evidence for legal purposes.

---

## Types of Digital Forensics Investigations

### 1. Public-Sector Investigations

These investigations are conducted by government agencies and law enforcement organizations. They are usually related to criminal or civil cases.

### 2. Private-Sector Investigations

These investigations are performed by corporations or private investigators and are generally initiated due to violations of company policies or internal security incidents.

---

## Importance of Digital Forensics

Digital forensic investigators are responsible for:

* Collecting digital evidence properly.
* Preserving evidence without altering it.
* Transporting devices securely.
* Analyzing large amounts of data stored on digital devices.
* Reconstructing events to determine what happened.

Without proper forensic procedures, digital evidence may become inadmissible or lose its integrity.

---

## Potential Sources of Digital Evidence

Digital devices that may contain valuable evidence include:

* Desktop computers
* Laptops
* Smartphones
* Tablets
* Digital cameras
* USB flash drives
* CDs and DVDs
* SD cards
* External storage devices
* Music players

These devices can contain emails, documents, photos, videos, browsing history, logs, and other information that may assist an investigation.
