# Task 1: Introduction

Forensic imaging is the process of creating an exact bit-by-bit copy of a storage device. This ensures that all data, including deleted files, hidden files, and unallocated space, is preserved for analysis.

The primary goal of forensic imaging is to create a reliable and verifiable copy while keeping the original evidence untouched. This helps maintain the integrity of digital evidence and supports legal investigations.

Additionally, forensic imaging plays an important role in maintaining the **Chain of Custody**, ensuring that evidence has not been altered or tampered with during handling.

## Learning Objectives

* Understand the basics of forensic imaging.
* Explore different imaging environments.
* Learn how to create a raw image from a storage device.
* Verify image integrity using integrity checks.
